What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyberattack where criminals attempt to trick you into revealing personal information, such as passwords, credit card numbers, or company works. They often use deceptive emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from legitimate organizations. It combines social engineering and technical trickery.

Common Phishing Techniques

  • Spoofed Emails: Emails that look like they're from trusted sources but are actually fake.
  • Fake Websites: Websites designed to mimic legitimate sites to steal your information.
  • Smishing (SMS Phishing): Phishing attacks conducted via text messages.
  • Vishing (Voice Phishing): Phishing attacks conducted via phone calls.

How to Protect Yourself

Warning: Be cautious of unsolicited emails or messages asking for personal information.
  • Verify Sender Identity: Always double-check the sender's email address or phone number.
  • Don't Click Suspicious Links: Hover over links to see their actual destination before clicking.
  • Be Wary of Urgent Requests: Phishers often create a sense of urgency to pressure you.
  • Use Strong Passwords: Create unique, complex passwords for your accounts.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Add an extra layer of security to your accounts.
  • Keep Software Updated: Regularly update your operating system and applications.
  • Educate Yourself: Stay informed about the latest phishing tactics.

What to Do If You've Been Phished

If you suspect you've been a victim of phishing, take immediate action:

  • Change your passwords.
  • Contact your bank or financial institution.
  • Report the incident to the appropriate authorities.
  • Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity.
